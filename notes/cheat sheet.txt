The following is the syntax for the key elements and concepts covered in the first nine chapters of the sources:

### Chapter 1: Structure
*   **Root Element:** `<html>...</html>` wraps all HTML code.
*   **Metadata Container:** `<head>...</head>` contains information about the page.
*   **Content Container:** `<body>...</body>` contains everything displayed in the browser.
*   **Attributes:** Appear on opening tags as name/value pairs: `<p lang="en-us">...</p>`.

### Chapter 2: Text
*   **Headings:** `<h1>...</h1>` (main) through `<h6>...</h6>` (least important).
*   **Paragraphs:** `<p>...</p>`.
*   **Bold & Italic (Structural):** `<b>...</b>` (bold) and `<i>...</i>` (italic).
*   **Emphasis & Importance (Semantic):** `<em>...</em>` (emphasis) and `<strong>...</strong>` (strong importance).
*   **Superscript & Subscript:** `<sup>...</sup>` and `<sub>...</sub>`.
*   **Line Breaks & Rules:** `<br />` (forced line break) and `<hr />` (horizontal rule).
*   **Quotations:** `<blockquote>...</blockquote>` (long) and `<q>...</q>` (short).
*   **Abbreviations:** `<abbr title="Full Name">Abbr</abbr>`.
*   **Citations & Definitions:** `<cite>...</cite>` and `<dfn>...</dfn>`.
*   **Edits:** `<ins>...</ins>` (inserted text) and `<del>...</del>` (deleted text).

### Chapter 3: Lists
*   **Ordered List:** `<ol><li>Item</li></ol>` (numbered).
*   **Unordered List:** `<ul><li>Item</li></ul>` (bulleted).
*   **Definition List:** `<dl><dt>Term</dt><dd>Definition</dd></dl>`.

### Chapter 4: Links
*   **Basic Link:** `<a href="URL">Link Text</a>`.
*   **New Window:** `<a href="URL" target="_blank">...</a>`.
*   **Email Link:** `<a href="mailto:email@example.com">...</a>`.
*   **Internal Page Link:** `<a href="#id-name">...</a>` targeting an element with `id="id-name"`.

### Chapter 5: Images
*   **Image Tag:** `<img src="path" alt="description" title="tooltip" />`.
*   **Sizing:** Attributes `width="n"` and `height="n"` specify dimensions in pixels.
*   **HTML5 Captions:** `<figure><img ... /><figcaption>Caption text</figcaption></figure>`.

### Chapter 6: Tables
*   **Table Structure:** `<table>` contains `<tr>` (rows), which contain `<th>` (headings) or `<td>` (data cells).
*   **Spanning:** `<td colspan="n">` (across columns) or `<td rowspan="n">` (across rows).
*   **Sections:** `<thead>`, `<tbody>`, and `<tfoot>` group rows for layout.

### Chapter 7: Forms
*   **Form Container:** `<form action="URL" method="get/post">...</form>`.
*   **Text Inputs:** `<input type="text" />` and `<input type="password" />`.
*   **Choices:** `<input type="radio" />` (one option) and `<input type="checkbox" />` (multiple).
*   **Drop-downs:** `<select><option value="v">Label</option></select>`.
*   **Multi-line Text:** `<textarea rows="n" cols="n">...</textarea>`.
*   **Submit Button:** `<input type="submit" value="Text" />` or `<button>...</button>`.
*   **Accessibility Labels:** `<label for="input-id">Text</label>` linked to `<input id="input-id" />`.
*   **Grouping:** `<fieldset><legend>Title</legend>...</fieldset>`.

### Chapter 8: Extra Markup
*   **DOCTYPE:** `<!DOCTYPE html>` defines the HTML version.
*   **Comments:** `<!-- Comment Text -->`.
*   **Global IDs & Classes:** Attributes `id="unique"` (for one element) and `class="group"` (for many).
*   **Containers:** `<div>...</div>` (block-level) and `<span>...</span>` (inline).
*   **Iframes:** `<iframe src="URL" width="n" height="n"></iframe>`.
*   **Meta Info:** `<meta name="description" content="Text" />`.
*   **Escape Characters:** Special codes like `&lt;` (<), `&gt;` (>), and `&copy;` (©).

### Chapter 9: Video & Audio
*   **Video:** `<video src="path" controls poster="img.jpg">...</video>`.
*   **Audio:** `<audio src="path" controls>...</audio>`.
*   **Multiple Sources:** Used inside video/audio to provide fallback formats: `<source src="path" type="video/mp4" />`.

***

**Analogy for Understanding Syntax:**
Think of HTML syntax like **punctuation and formatting in a letter**. The **tags** are like the envelopes and headings that tell the post office (the browser) what kind of information is inside. **Attributes** are like the specific delivery instructions written on the envelope—such as "Fragile" (the `alt` text) or "Deliver to Back Door" (the `target` attribute). Every tag must be properly opened and closed, just as every sentence must start with a capital letter and end with a period, or the message might be misunderstood.
